# STAT1092-Project-Research-2026
Statistical Analysis of Queueing Behavior at An OPD Ticket Counte
Queueing theory is a branch of applied probability and statistics that deals with the
mathematical study of waiting lines or queues. It helps analyze systems in which customers
or patients arrive for service, wait in a queue if the service facility is busy, and depart after
receiving service. Queueing models are widely used in healthcare, banking, transportation,
telecommunication, and manufacturing systems to evaluate performance measures such as
waiting time, queue length, server utilization, and system efficiency.
In healthcare institutions, especially in Outpatient Department (OPD) service counters,
efficient patient management is extremely important because large patient arrivals and
limited service facilities often lead to congestion and long waiting times. Excessive waiting
not only affects patient satisfaction but also reduces the operational efficiency of the
hospital system. Therefore, statistical and queueing analysis of OPD services can help
identify bottlenecks and improve resource allocation and service quality.
The present project focuses on the analysis of the OPD counter service system at
Barasat Government Medical College and Hospital. Real-life arrival and service data were
collected from the OPD counters of the hospital to study the behavior of the queueing
process. The primary objective of this study is to model the patient arrival and service
mechanisms using suitable probabilistic and statistical methods and to determine an
appropriate queueing model for the system.
In this project, special attention is given to the stochastic behavior of inter-arrival and
service times. The study examines whether classical assumptions such as Poisson arrivals
and exponential service times are valid for the observed data. Since healthcare systems
often show time-dependent and irregular arrival patterns, alternative distributions and
stochastic processes are also explored. Statistical tools such as exploratory data analysis,
graphical visualization, goodness-of-fit tests, and distribution fitting techniques are used
to analyze the collected data.
The project further investigates important queueing characteristics including arrival
rate, service rate, server utilization, traffic intensity, and waiting behavior of patients in
the OPD counter system. The analysis is performed using statistical software and compu-
tational methods to provide both theoretical and practical insights into the functioning of
the hospital service process.
The findings of this study may help improve patient flow management and reduce
congestion in OPD services by providing a better understanding of the queue dynamics.
Overall, this project demonstrates the practical application of queueing theory and
statistical modeling in analyzing and improving real-world healthcare service systems.
